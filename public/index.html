Funding Opportunity Scoring

Where is the money, who is eligible, and what is the competitive landscape?

Each county receives a composite Funding Opportunity Score (0-100) based on six weighted factors that identify the best broadband investment targets:

  • Coverage Gap (25%) — % of BSLs underserved or unserved (sqrt-scaled to spread real-data distribution)
  • Unserved % (20%) — % of BSLs below 25/3 Mbps (sqrt-scaled)
  • Funding Eligibility (20%) — BEAD approval status × unserved BSL concentration
  • Income Need (15%) — Lower median income = higher grant eligibility (continuous scale)
  • Population Density (10%) — Sweet spot: 50-200/sq mi (buildable, not saturated)
  • Infrastructure Readiness (10%) — Provider count, fiber penetration, density factor

Data Sources

  • BSL / Coverage: FCC Broadband Data Collection (BDC) via ArcGIS Living Atlas — real county-level data for all 3,100+ counties
  • Demographics: US Census ACS 5-Year Estimates (2022) — population, income, poverty
  • Geography: Census TIGERweb — county land area and coordinates
  • Funding: NTIA BEAD Allocations (June 2023) — $42.45B across all states
  • CBRS: FCC ULS Database — PAL license counts and incumbent exclusion zones
  • Smart Cities: Smart Cities Council, city government reports, public press releases
